## 1. Overview

The **Algorithm Scores** are predictive metrics generated by **Ask Studio (YouTube AI)** based on your channel's historical performance data. These scores help predict how well a planned video will perform with YouTube's recommendation algorithm.

These values are **NOT calculated locally** - they are AI predictions from YouTube's own systems, returned as part of the content plan JSON.

---

## 2. Data Source

### 2.1 Request Format

In `yppService.ts`, we request Ask Studio to include algorithm scores in its response:


## 4. Detailed Parameter Definitions

### 4.1 PIS (Pattern Interrupt Score)

**Definition:** Measures the effectiveness of the video's opening hook in the first 0-0.3 seconds.

**What It Evaluates:**
- Visual shock value (sudden color changes, unexpected motion)
- Audio interruption (startling sound effects)
- Information density (how quickly key info is presented)
- "Stop the scroll" effectiveness

**Scoring Thresholds:**
| Score | Rating | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| â‰¥90 | ğŸŸ¢ Excellent | Extremely strong hook, minimal swipe-away |
| 80-89 | ğŸŸ¡ Good | Solid hook, room for improvement |
| <80 | ğŸ”´ Needs Work | Opening is weak, high swipe-away risk |

**Algorithm Impact:** 
- High PIS directly correlates with low swipe-away rate
- Swipe-away in first 0.5s kills algorithmic push entirely

**Optimization Strategies:**
1. Start with visual flash or dramatic action
2. Use unexpected camera angles
3. Avoid slow fades or text-only openings
4. Sound design: Start with impact sound, not silence

---

### 4.2 3s Retention (3-Second Retention Rate)

**Definition:** Predicted percentage of viewers who will watch past the first 3 seconds.

**YouTube Algorithm Importance:** **#1 CRITICAL METRIC**

The 3-second mark is YouTube's primary evaluation point for Shorts. If retention drops here, the video is immediately deprioritized.

**Calculation Factors:**
- Channel's historical 3s retention average
- Content type match with audience preferences
- Title/thumbnail attractiveness
- Hook quality (linked to PIS)

**Scoring Thresholds:**
| Score | Rating | Algorithm Response |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| â‰¥75% | ğŸŸ¢ Excellent | Strong push to Shorts Feed |
| 60-74% | ğŸŸ¡ Moderate | Limited distribution |
| <60% | ğŸ”´ Poor | Content suppressed |

**Target:** â‰¥70% for algorithm favor (per `yppService.ts` line 560)

**Optimization Strategies:**
1. Front-load the conflict/payoff tease in first 3 seconds
2. Avoid "setup" phases - go straight to action
3. Use text overlays that create curiosity
4. Match content style to channel niche

2. Build tension continuously - no "dead" moments
3. Save the biggest payoff for the final 1-2 seconds
4. Avoid repetitive content that causes early exit

### 4.4 Loop Rate (Predicted Rewatch/Loop Rate)

**Definition:** Predicted percentage of viewers who will watch the video more than once.

**YouTube Algorithm Importance:** **#1 VIRALITY INDICATOR**

Loop rate is the single most important metric for Shorts virality. A high loop rate signals to YouTube that the content is extraordinarily engaging.

**What Causes Looping:**
- "Wait, what just happened?" moments
- Hidden details discovered on rewatch
- Satisfying visual loops (end â†’ beginning seamlessness)
- Cliffhanger resolution that creates curiosity

**Scoring Thresholds:**
| Score | Rating | Virality Potential |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| â‰¥50% | ğŸŸ¢ Viral | Extreme push potential |
| 35-49% | ğŸŸ¡ Good | Solid organic growth |
| <35% | ğŸ”´ Low | Limited viral chance |

**Target:** â‰¥40% loop rate (per `yppService.ts` line 560)

**The Loop Trigger Technique:**
In the promptBlock structure, the "LOOP TRIGGER" segment (6.0-7.0s) is specifically designed to:
1. Create a visual callback to the opening hook
2. End on a moment that makes viewers want to rewatch
3. Include a "Did you notice...?" element

**Optimization Strategies:**
1. End with a freeze-frame that connects to opening
2. Include subtle details only visible on rewatch
3. Use circular narratives (end ties to beginning)
4. Create "wait for it" moments that reward looping

### 4.5 CQ (Controversy Quotient)

**Definition:** Measures the potential of content to generate discussion, debate, or strong reactions.

**Range:** 0.0 (completely neutral) to 1.0 (highly controversial)

**Scoring Interpretation:**
| Score | Rating | Engagement Style |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| â‰¥0.7 | ğŸŸ  High | Polarizing, high comment rate, some risk |
| 0.4-0.6 | ğŸŸ¡ Moderate | Balanced engagement, safe |
| <0.4 | âšª Low | Safe but may lack interaction |

**Algorithm Impact:**
- Higher CQ â†’ Higher comment rate â†’ Better engagement signals
- But: Too high CQ can trigger content moderation or negative reactions

**What Increases CQ:**
- Opinion-based content
- "This vs That" comparisons
- Unexpected outcomes
- Calling out common beliefs

**Optimization Balance:**
- Target CQ of 0.5-0.7 for optimal engagement without risk
- Avoid controversial topics that violate platform guidelines
- Use "soft controversy" (surprising facts, not political opinions)
